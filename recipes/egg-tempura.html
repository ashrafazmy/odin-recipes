Egg-Tempura Don

Description

A frozen egg that is deep-fried in tempura batter, producing a soft-boiled tempura egg over rice. Though the dish uses common, low-grade eggs, the dishes' power comes from this fact as no other egg grade can produce the flavor that dish can create. Despite its low grade ingredients, it was sufficient enough to please Erina's God's Tongue.

Ingredients

For 2 people

  • 2 Bowls of Cooked Rice

  • 4 Eggs

  • 6 Tablespoons Cold Water

Ingredient Part A

  • 5 Tablespoons Weak Flour

  • 1 Tablespoon Potato Starch

Ingredient Part B

  • 100cc Bonito Broth

  • 3 Tablespoon Soy Sauce

  • 2 Tablespoons each Mirin, Sugar

Steps

  1. Freeze the eggs, once completely frozen, peel.

  2. Pour Ingredient Part A into a bowl and whisk together slowly adding the cold water while whisking until blended into a smooth batter.

  3. Dust the eggs from (1.) with flour. Dip them into the batter from (2.) and then deep-fry for 6 minutes in frying oil. Heated to 320Â°F. Optionally, dip the face of shiso leaves in the batter from (2.) and deep-fry until crispy.

  4. Pour Ingredient Part B into a small saucepan. Mix together and let simmer for about 3 minutes.

  5. Fill 2 bowls with cooked rice. Pour a dash of sauce from (4.) over the top. Arrange the eggs and shiso from (3.) on top of the rice. Pour more sauce over the top, and done!
